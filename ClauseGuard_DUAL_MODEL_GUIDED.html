You need only two things: a Gemini API key from Google and the complete policy text. ClauseGuard then reads the agreement, generates its own findings, and links each finding back to the source wording.

1

Get a Gemini API key from Google

Open Google AI Studio, sign in, choose Create API key, and copy the generated key. New keys are tied to a Google Cloud project. Availability, free quotas, and billing depend on Google’s current model and account rules.

Open Google AI Studio ↗Official key guide ↗Pricing & free tier ↗
Keep it private. Treat an API key like a password. ClauseGuard does not receive it, but Direct mode must send it from your browser to Google to authenticate requests. Do not share screenshots containing the key.
2

Paste the key into ClauseGuard

Open API settings, paste the key, choose a model, keep Direct to Google Gemini API, and press Save.

  • Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite: recommended when accuracy matters most.
  • Gemma 4 31B: alternative for heavier usage or quota flexibility.
  • Leave “Remember” off on shared devices.
3

Find and add the complete policy

On the service’s website, look in the footer, sign-up page, checkout, subscription page, or account settings for links named Terms, Privacy, User Agreement, EULA, or Subscription Terms.

  • Open every linked document that is incorporated into the agreement.
  • Copy the full text and paste it into ClauseGuard, or upload a TXT, HTML, Markdown, or text-based PDF.
  • Do not paste only the suspicious paragraph—the surrounding definitions and exceptions affect severity.
4

Analyze and review the evidence

Press Analyze agreement. ClauseGuard reads each section twice, performs a full-document calibration pass, verifies quotes locally, and opens the review workspace.

5

Understand the privacy boundary

ClauseGuard is a standalone browser file and does not operate a collection backend. In Direct mode, your key and policy are sent to Google’s Gemini API—not to ClauseGuard. Unpaid and paid API tiers can have different data-use terms, so avoid uploading secrets unless your Google setup is appropriate.

Legal & privacy

Effective July 14, 2026 · Standalone test build

Plain-language privacy promise

ClauseGuard does not run accounts, ads, analytics, tracking pixels, cookies, or a developer API in this standalone file. The operator of this build does not receive your pasted documents or Gemini API key. Your browser processes the interface locally, then sends data only to the connection you choose.

Privacy policy and API-key handling

API keys. The key is held in page memory and, depending on your setting, browser session storage or local storage. It is not included in reports, printouts, copied summaries, or requests to a configured proxy. In Direct mode it is transmitted over HTTPS in the x-goog-api-key header only to Google’s Gemini API endpoint so Google can authenticate your request.

Documents. Pasted and uploaded files are parsed in your browser. In Direct mode, the relevant document text and analysis instructions are sent to Google. In Proxy mode, they are sent to the proxy URL you provide. ClauseGuard cannot control or promise the privacy practices of Google, your proxy, browser extensions, device administrators, hosting providers, or modified copies of this file.

Local storage. ClauseGuard may store connection mode, proxy URL, sidebar width, review progress, and—only when you choose it—the API key in browser storage. It does not intentionally use cookies. Use “Clear ClauseGuard data” below to remove ClauseGuard-prefixed local and session data from this browser.

No sale or sharing by ClauseGuard. This standalone build does not sell, rent, monetize, or disclose your API key or document text to the developer. Ordinary network providers may still process technical connection data, and Google or your selected proxy processes the API request.

Google Gemini API disclosure

Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite and hosted Gemma 4 31B are Google services accessed through the Gemini API. Your use is subject to Google’s current Gemini API terms, privacy policy, prohibited-use rules, regional availability, billing, quota, model availability, and key-security requirements.

Google’s terms distinguish paid and unpaid services. Google warns users not to submit sensitive, confidential, or personal information to unpaid services because submitted content and generated responses may be used to improve Google products and may be reviewed by humans. For paid services, Google states that prompts and responses are not used to improve its products, subject to its terms and limited safety logging.

Google currently states that prompts, contextual information, and outputs may be retained for 55 days for abuse monitoring and related legal or regulatory disclosures. Google’s terms also currently require API users to be at least 18, describe the API as intended for professional or business development rather than consumer use, and impose regional and paid-service conditions.

AI, evidence, and rating limitations

AI can omit clauses, misunderstand definitions, misread cross-references, hallucinate, overstate risk, understate risk, or produce inconsistent results. Source matching only shows that similar wording was found; it does not prove the interpretation is legally correct.

The deterministic risk score is a product heuristic, not a legal rating, certification, safety guarantee, fraud determination, enforceability opinion, or prediction of what a court or regulator will decide. An unverified quote contributes no score, but verified wording can still be interpreted incorrectly.

Scanned PDFs, damaged files, tables, unusual formatting, images, multiple languages, incorporated documents, missing schedules, and very long policies may produce partial analysis. Always read the full original agreement and linked documents.
